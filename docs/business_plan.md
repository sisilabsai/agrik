BUSINESS PLAN FOR AGRIK (AGRICULTURE GREEN INNOVATION KNOWLEDGE)
1. Executive Summary
AGRIK is an agritech and agri-finance project from SIMON SHARP PRODUCTS LIMIED focused on transforming smallholder farming in rural and underserved areas through insurance, agroecology, and digital agricultural extension. The project leverages artificial intelligence, geospatial intelligence, and multilingual digital channels to deliver real-time, predictive, and personalized agricultural advisory services, while simultaneously enabling financial inclusion through data-driven insurance and credit solutions.
AGRIK’s core platform (GRIK AI) functions as a digital agricultural intelligence infrastructure, an AI-powered extension officer accessible on any phone, from basic feature phones to smartphones. By integrating soil, weather, crop, pest, and market data, AGRIK enables farmers to make informed decisions, reduce climate and production risks, increase yields, and improve incomes.
The company targets smallholder farmers, farmer groups, cooperatives, insurers, agribusinesses, NGOs, and governments, beginning in Uganda and scaling across Africa and other developing regions.

3. Problem Statement
Smallholder farmers in Uganda and similar developing economies face multiple structural challenges:
•	Unpredictable climate change impacts (droughts, floods, erratic rainfall)
•	Late detection of pests and diseases
•	Severe shortage of extension officers
•	Language and literacy barriers
•	Generic, non-location-specific farming advice
•	Poor access to reliable and timely market price information
•	Exclusion from agricultural insurance and formal credit systems
•	Luck of market access through digital platforms 
Existing digital agriculture solutions are often: - English-only - Smartphone-dependent - Reactive rather than predictive - Expensive or donor-dependent - Not integrated with insurance or finance
This leaves millions of farmers making high-risk decisions without reliable intelligence or financial protection.

5. Core Technology & Innovation Pillars
5.1 Real-Time Multilingual Conversational AI(GRIK)
•	Farmers interact in local languages (e.g., Luganda, Runyankole, Ateso, Luo)
•	GRIK AI diagnoses crop issues and provides actionable solutions instantly
•	Context-aware memory builds farmer-specific intelligence over time
•	Operates without human intervention, enabling scale
5.2 GRIK AI Voice Agricultural Assistant
•	Farmers call a dedicated number and speak naturally
•	Ideal for elderly farmers and those with limited literacy
•	Provides step-by-step agroecological and climate-smart practices
5.3 Hyperlocal Geospatial Intelligence
•	Satellite and weather data integration
•	Parish-level rainfall and weather predictions
•	Soil suitability and vegetation health analysis (NDVI)
•	Location-specific advisory instead of regional generalizations
5.4 Predictive Pest & Disease Surveillance
•	Aggregates data from farmer reports, images, weather, and crop cycles
•	Detects early warning signs of outbreaks
•	Sends geo-targeted alerts before widespread damage occurs
5.5 Digital Farm Memory (Farmer Profiles)
Each farmer has a living digital record including: - Crops grown and planting dates - Soil and weather history - Past pest and disease incidents - Yield and performance trends
This continuously improves advisory accuracy and risk assessment.
