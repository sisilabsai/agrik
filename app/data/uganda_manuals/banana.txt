UGANDA EXTENSION MANUAL (EXPANDED)
CROP: BANANA / MATOKE (Musa spp.)

LOCAL VARIETAL NOTES (VERIFY LOCALLY)
- Cooking bananas (matooke) and dessert types are grown; select varieties recommended for your zone.
- Confirm current recommended varieties with district extension offices or research stations.

REGIONAL DIFFERENCES (UGANDA)
- Lake basin and central areas: favorable for banana production with good moisture.
- Western highlands: manage for cooler temperatures and disease pressure.

SITE AND SOIL
- Deep, fertile, well-drained soils; apply mulch to conserve moisture.

PLANTING MATERIAL
- Use clean planting material (tissue culture or healthy suckers).

SPACING
- 2.5 m x 2.5 m for cooking bananas (adjust by system).

PEST AND DISEASE SYMPTOM GUIDE
- Banana weevil: weak plants, damaged corms, lodging.
- Xanthomonas wilt: yellowing, bacterial ooze, premature fruit ripening.
- Nematodes: reduced root mass, poor anchorage.

MANAGEMENT (IPM)
- Remove and destroy infected plants.
- Keep plantations clean and well-mulched.

HARVEST
- Harvest when fingers are full and well-rounded.
