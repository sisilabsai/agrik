UGANDA EXTENSION MANUAL (EXPANDED)
CROP: CASSAVA (Manihot esculenta)

LOCAL VARIETAL NOTES (VERIFY LOCALLY)
- Improved, disease-tolerant varieties are preferred where available.
- District extension offices and NARO listings provide current recommended varieties by zone.

REGIONAL DIFFERENCES (UGANDA)
- Northern and Eastern regions: prioritize early-maturing and drought-tolerant varieties due to longer dry spells.
- Central region: focus on high-yield varieties with good root quality for fresh and processing markets.
- Western highlands: plant on well-drained slopes; manage erosion and waterlogging carefully.

SITE SELECTION
- Well-drained soils; avoid waterlogging and low-lying flood zones.

PLANTING MATERIAL
- Use healthy, disease-free cuttings (20-25 cm long) from mature plants.
- Select cuttings with 5-7 nodes and plant at an angle or vertical in moist soil.

PLANTING
- Plant at onset of rains.
- Spacing: 1 m x 1 m for most varieties; adjust for intercropping.

SOIL FERTILITY
- Apply compost or well-decomposed manure at planting.
- Maintain ground cover or mulch where possible.

PEST AND DISEASE SYMPTOM GUIDE
- Cassava mosaic disease: mottled or twisted leaves, stunted growth, reduced root size.
- Cassava brown streak disease: yellowing/chlorosis on leaves, brown necrosis in roots.
- Mealybugs: white cottony masses on shoots, leaf curling and stunting.
- Green mites: fine yellow speckling on leaves, leaf drop in severe cases.

MANAGEMENT (IPM)
- Rogue and destroy infected plants early.
- Use resistant varieties where available.
- Control weeds and avoid movement of infected cuttings between fields.

HARVEST
- Harvest 9-12 months after planting depending on variety and market need.
- Avoid bruising roots; process or dry quickly after harvest.
