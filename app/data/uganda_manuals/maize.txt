UGANDA EXTENSION MANUAL (EXPANDED)
CROP: MAIZE (Zea mays)

LOCAL VARIETAL NOTES (VERIFY LOCALLY)
- Hybrid and OPV (open-pollinated) options exist; choose varieties recommended for your agro-ecological zone.
- Confirm current recommended varieties with district extension offices or NARO releases.

REGIONAL DIFFERENCES (UGANDA)
- Eastern and Northern regions: favor early-maturing and drought-tolerant varieties.
- Central region: prioritize high-yield hybrids with good storage quality.
- Western highlands: ensure spacing and disease management for cooler, wetter conditions.

LAND PREPARATION
- Use well-drained soils; avoid waterlogged areas.
- Plough or dig to fine tilth before planting.

PLANTING
- Plant at the onset of rains.
- Spacing: 75 cm between rows and 25-30 cm between plants.
- Plant 2 seeds per hole; thin to 1 plant after emergence.

SOIL FERTILITY
- Apply compost/manure at planting.
- If using fertilizer, apply basal NPK and top-dress with urea at 3-4 weeks.

WEED MANAGEMENT
- Weed early, especially in the first 6 weeks.

PEST AND DISEASE SYMPTOM GUIDE
- Fall armyworm: windowed leaves, frass in whorl, ragged leaf edges.
- Maize streak virus: yellow streaks on leaves, stunted growth.
- Stem borers: dead heart (central leaf dies), entry holes with frass.

MANAGEMENT (IPM)
- Scout weekly and remove egg masses early.
- Rotate with legumes to reduce pest pressure.

HARVEST
- Harvest when cobs are dry and grains are hard.
- Dry to safe moisture before storage.
