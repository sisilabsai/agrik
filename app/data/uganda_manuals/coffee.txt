UGANDA EXTENSION MANUAL (EXPANDED)
CROP: COFFEE (Coffea arabica / robusta)

LOCAL VARIETAL NOTES (VERIFY LOCALLY)
- Arabica is common in higher altitudes; Robusta in lower altitudes.
- Confirm current recommended varieties with UCDA or district extension offices.

REGIONAL DIFFERENCES (UGANDA)
- Eastern highlands (Mt Elgon) and western highlands: Arabica favored.
- Central and western lowlands: Robusta favored.

SITE AND SOIL
- Well-drained loam soils with shade are ideal.
- Avoid waterlogging.

PLANTING
- Plant at onset of rains.
- Spacing: 2.5 m x 2.5 m (varies by system).

NUTRITION
- Apply organic manure and mulch regularly.
- Maintain soil fertility with balanced nutrients.

PEST AND DISEASE SYMPTOM GUIDE
- Coffee wilt disease: sudden wilting and death.
- Coffee berry disease: black lesions on berries.
- Leaf rust: orange powdery spots under leaves.

MANAGEMENT (IPM)
- Prune regularly and remove diseased branches.
- Use resistant varieties where available.

HARVEST
- Pick ripe red cherries only.
- Dry and store in clean, dry conditions.
