UGANDA EXTENSION MANUAL (EXPANDED)
CROP: BEANS (Phaseolus vulgaris)

LOCAL VARIETAL NOTES (VERIFY LOCALLY)
- Bush and climbing types are common; select varieties recommended for your zone.
- Confirm current recommended varieties with district extension offices or NARO releases.

REGIONAL DIFFERENCES (UGANDA)
- Highlands (e.g., Kigezi, Mt Elgon): climbing beans perform well with staking.
- Lower elevations: bush beans are commonly preferred for shorter cycles.

LAND AND SOIL
- Well-drained soils; avoid waterlogging.
- Incorporate organic matter before planting.

PLANTING
- Plant with onset of rains.
- Spacing: 50 cm between rows and 10-15 cm between plants.

WEEDING
- First weeding 2-3 weeks after emergence.
- Second weeding before flowering.

PEST AND DISEASE SYMPTOM GUIDE
- Angular leaf spot: brown angular lesions on leaves, defoliation.
- Root rots: yellowing, stunting, poor root development.
- Bean fly: stem lesions near soil line, seedlings wilt.

MANAGEMENT (IPM)
- Rotate with cereals to reduce disease pressure.
- Avoid working fields when plants are wet.

HARVEST AND STORAGE
- Harvest when pods are dry.
- Dry grain well and store in clean, dry containers.
