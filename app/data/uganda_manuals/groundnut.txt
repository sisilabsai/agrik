UGANDA EXTENSION MANUAL (EXPANDED)
CROP: GROUNDNUT (Arachis hypogaea)

LOCAL VARIETAL NOTES (VERIFY LOCALLY)
- Commonly grown types include early-maturing and drought-tolerant varieties.
- Confirm current recommended varieties with district extension offices or NARO releases.

REGIONAL DIFFERENCES (UGANDA)
- Northern and Eastern regions: prioritize drought-tolerant varieties and early planting.
- Central region: manage for higher humidity to reduce leaf spot pressure.

SOIL AND LAND
- Light, well-drained soils; avoid heavy clay soils.

PLANTING
- Plant at start of rains.
- Spacing: 45-60 cm between rows, 10-15 cm within rows.

WEEDS
- Weed early; avoid disturbing pegs after flowering.

PEST AND DISEASE SYMPTOM GUIDE
- Leaf spot: brown spots with yellow halos, leaf drop.
- Aphids: curling leaves, sticky honeydew.
- Aflatoxin risk: moldy kernels, poor drying, storage in damp conditions.

MANAGEMENT (IPM)
- Rotate with cereals and ensure good field sanitation.
- Dry quickly to reduce aflatoxin risk.

HARVEST
- Harvest when most pods are mature and shells are firm.
- Dry quickly and store in dry conditions.
